Designing unite

Client
3Shape
Service
UI Design
I was tasked with designing the webpages for the launch of 3Shape Unite, a new collaborative dental platform. As the UI designer, I was responsible for creating interactive pages with a nice UI animation. I also wanted to focus on the story of the product and its benefits to the clients of dentists who own this scanner.
Once I had a good understanding of the target audience, I began sketching out some ideas for the webpages. I wanted to create a design that was visually appealing and easy to navigate. I also wanted to make sure that the pages were packed with information about 3Shape Unite, but that the information was presented in a way that was easy to understand.

One open dental platform. Workflows that just flow.

In addition to the visual elements, I also focused on the story of the product. I wanted to create a webpage that would educate users about 3Shape Unite and its potential benefits. I also wanted to create a sense of timeline in the story. That's why I have a scrolling timeline on the page as main technique.

The Design Process

I worked on two different design versions for the webpages. The first version was more traditional, with a clean and organized layout. The second version was more playful and interactive, with a variety of animations and visual effects. I presented both design versions to the key stakeholders, and they were torn between the two. They liked the simplicity and clarity of the first version, but they were also drawn to the excitement and energy of the second version.
The decision was eventually escalated to the marketing director, who decided to go with the first version after discussing it with us. He felt that the vertical and more conservative design was a safe choice. I was a little disappointed that my second design version wasn't chosen, but I understood the marketing director's decision. I'm proud of both design versions, and I'm glad that I was able to contribute to the successful launch of 3Shape Unite.
