3Shape is a Danish company that makes best-in-class intraoral scanners, lab scanners, and software for dentists. They won 6 Red Dot awards for their products.

I worked for many times on this project in different periods. The main website has many challenges and one of them is a lot of stakeholders that are involved in decision-making. Another thing is 7-9 languages for localization content. Also, integrations with every other platform in the 3Shape family.

The task specifics

Task: A professional and modern design that matches the level of 3Shape's products; A user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate; A focus on the products and services of the company; A mobile-friendly design; A lead-generation system to capture contact information from potential customer; localized content to 7-9 languages.
The original task was to design a new website that would match the level of their products. Also, the website will be the main lead-generative platform for new consumers who want to buy 3Shape products. The old website was too bad and couldn't handle the new and challenging demands of growth.

Language selector

At some point, I was given a task to redesign a current language selector because many stakeholders expressed a desire to improve its UX. The complexity of the job was: 3-5 platforms with 7-9 languages on each that should have the same unified Country selector with some small local differences. The decision was to make it not as intrusive as the previous one and avoid asking about the locations of the existing users. A side panel was a good option for it.
I did the whole project from UX research, prototype, and usability testing to the final high-fidelity designs.

TRIOS Services

I tested the prototype with users of our initial ideas and in the end, we decided to totally change our approach because the feedback was not pleasing. We went with simple FAQ pages but added some fun images, part of the global campaign in social media.
Sometime before the Country Selector I had an opportunity to do the user testing for the TRIOS Services project.
The problem was that users were quite informed about the company's scanners but not about the software part of it. So business decided to create a special promo page for software.
